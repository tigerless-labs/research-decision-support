# [Method] Zettelkasten -- fleeting notes are discarded after use

Niklas Luhmann. Empirical record of one person: roughly 90,000 cards and 70-plus books in a
lifetime. Popularized by Ahrens, "How to Take Smart Notes".

Core logic: three kinds of notes with strict division of labor -- fleeting (captured on the fly,
**discarded after processing**), literature (what the source said), permanent (my own claims:
atomic, self-contained, interlinked). Two key points: fleeting notes are a disposable buffer, not
a collection; the voices of literature and permanent notes never share a card (what the source
says != what I say).

Boundaries: the labor of maintaining the permanent-note network falls entirely on the owner;
digital-age imitators mostly die of "capturing without processing" (see the
[notes graveyard](../blogs/notes-graveyard.md)).

**Relation to this project**: the origin of the legitimacy of inbox items evaporating (fleeting
means disposable); "three voices, different cards" is the precedent for separating evidence cards
from decision cards. The maintenance labor of permanent notes moves to the agent.
