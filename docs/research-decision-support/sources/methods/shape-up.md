# [Method] Shape Up -- no backlog, and the betting table

Ryan Singer / Basecamp (2019). Publicly published. Years of Basecamp practice.
[Bets, Not Backlogs](https://basecamp.com/shapeup/2.1-chapter-07).

Core logic: **the backlog is a time sink** -- the labor of grooming old ideas crowds out what
matters now, and any inventory that needs grooming inevitably rots. The replacement is the betting
table: only a handful of **shaped** options (risks converged, approach formed) sit on the table;
bet or let go, nothing to track. Raw ideas default to a **soft no**: "ideas are cheap", and truly
important ideas come back on their own -- when did you last forget a genuinely good idea? The word
"bet" is chosen deliberately, admitting the uncertainty of the decision.

Boundaries: shaping is carried by senior members, an upfront labor cost; rituals like the six-week
cycle are designed for team settings.

**Relation to this project**: the overall origin of the adjudication table -- immature decisions
are invisible and uncounted (curing "a chaotic pile of ideas"), only 2-4 shaped cards get served,
un-bet cards are let go automatically, and evaporation is legitimate. The shaping labor is carried
by the agent (gathering evidence + assembling the matrix), eliminating its upfront cost.
